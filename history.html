History

Before women started petitioning or even thinking about gaining equal rights there were a lot of problems with the way people percieved women. Women didn't have the right to have control of income and didn't even have the right to keep their children after a divorce. Through the three waves of feminism things changed in massive ways. If the ladies of the 19th century didn't believe that they deseved to have rights our world would be in a much different place.

The First Wave

• The first womens convention was held in Seneca Falls, New York

• 19th century gender roles : men dominated the public sphere by voting, working and participating in politics while women stayed home to cook, clean and raise children

• There were also a lot of restrictions on women voting, owning property and having control of income

• The women of Seneca Falls decided to hold a convention for the social, civil and religious rights of women which became known as the Seneca Falls Convention

• They wrote a Declaration of Sentiments stating the things that women deserved as humans and it was signed by all the attendees of the second day of the convention

• It was focused specifically on voting rights

• They were mocked all over the country by newspapers

• After decades of protesting the women’s suffrage finally resulted in the 19th amendment which gave women the right to vote

The Second Wave

• This wave started after women got the right to vote, and takes place after World War II

• Feminists participated in the civil rights movement, because they wanted more participation and equal pay in the workplace

• The Equal Pay Act was an attempt to combat this issue, but it is still ongoing

• The Feminine Mystique by Betty Friedan highlighted the fact that women are still confined to their roles as housewives

• The Roe v. Wade case decision in Supreme Court was a huge milestone, because it gave women the right to an abortion. However, there are still states that are banning abortion

The Third Wave

The third wave of Feminism is the most recent

This wave brings attention to prominent cases of sexual assault and “rape culture”

The #MeToo movement and women’s marches have provided feminists with a high-profile platforms for advocating on behalf of full rights for all women worldwide.
