Figures

Betty Friedan

Betty Friedan is a writer and activist who wrote The Feminine Mystique, which is credited for sparking the second wave of feminism. She established the National Women’s Political Caucus and also organized the Women’s Strike for Equality in 1970, which gave more attention to the feminist movement.

Gloria Steinem

Gloria Steinem is a feminist, journalist, and activist who was recognized as a spokesperson for the feminist movement in the late 1960’s and early 70’s. She was the founder of Ms. Magazine, and wrote for the New York Times. She was also given the Presidential Medal of Freedom for her monumental efforts for the movement.

Angela Davis

Angela Davis is a trailblazing voice for black women, because she was an advocate for the feminist and Civil Rights movements. She most recently served as an honorary co-chair for the Women’s March on Washington in 2017.

Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Ruth Bader Ginsburg is a Supreme Court Justice, and also co-founded the Women’s Rights Law Reporter in 1970, the first U.S. law journal to focus exclusively on women’s rights. She also co-founded the Women’s Rights Project at the American Civil Liberties Union.

Sheryl Sandberg

Sheryl Sandberg is Facebook’s COO and responsible for pioneering the “Lean In” movement with her book that encouraged women to excel in the workforce. She speaks about the inequality that women face in the workforce, and teamed up with Gloria Steinem to empower young girls.
